Exploration through collaboration


Mission

Fleet Science is a collaborative marine ecology research project, which will see the worlds cruising community work hand in hand with the world's scientific community. This platform is a global network of free resource for marine expedition research – A first of its kind “Exploration through Collaboration” - not crowd science but “Fleet Science!”​ By bridging the gap between sailors and marine scientists, data gathered by sailors offers marine scientists a starting point to beat the ocean's deterioration. Besides, the data gathered by sailors can be used to create public awareness about the changes of the oceans. The goal of the platform Fleet Science is therefore to provide a general interface for marine scientists, sailors and the public, where marine scientists can send data requests, sailors can upload data and the public can view the gathered data in a playful way.


Vision

The oceans cover 70 percent of the world's surface, produce over 50 percent of the oxygen, absorb 50 percent of the carbon produced and account for 80 percent of our biodiversity. Unfortunately, the oceans are changing continuously at alarming proportions, as a result of persistent climate change and human activities. The urgency of ocean preservation is acknowledged by renowned international institutions, which motivates the necessity to create public awareness about the changes of the oceans, and to explore innovative ways to provide marine scientists with data they need to study the oceans. To address this problem, the platform Fleet Science is established by a collaboration between the global sailor community and the global marine research community. Sailors who make round-the-world trips are able to gather data for marine scientists in a time efficient and cost-effective way, and in areas which are hardly accessed by traditional research vessels.


Want to get involved?

If you are a sailor, then please register your vessel and participate in data collection around the world. Please add as much details about your vessel and trip as possible for collaboration with the academics. Once you have registered one of our lead boats will review your request. After acceptance you can start sharing! If you are a member of the scientific community, a researcher, an environmentalist and you would like us to collect a specific piece of data, use a specific portable instrument or you want to be hosted on a fleet boat - please register as an academic and start creating data requests.
